Strong Password =
Strong Protection

Passwords are the first line of defence. Weak ones are cracked in seconds — strong ones keep attackers out and your accounts safe.

Why It Matters

How Fast Can a Password Be Cracked?

Automated tools can try millions of combinations per second. The difference between "123456" being cracked and "My@School2026!" is the difference between milliseconds and centuries.

❌ Weak Passwords
❌  123456
❌  password
❌  deepak123
❌  iloveyou

These are cracked in under 1 second by automated tools.

✅ Strong Passwords
✅  My@School2026!
✅  Cyber#Safe123
✅  G!r@f3-Jump$
✅  Sun$et_Over_007

These would take decades or longer for a computer to crack.

The Rules

What Makes a Password Strong?

1

Length: 8–12+ Characters

Longer passwords are exponentially harder to crack. Aim for at least 12 characters.

2

Mix Everything

Combine uppercase (A-Z), lowercase (a-z), numbers (0-9), and symbols (!@#$%^&*).

3

One Account, One Password

Never reuse passwords. If one account leaks, others stay safe.

4

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

A second verification step — like an OTP — means even a stolen password can't get in.

🗂️

Use a Password Manager

A password manager remembers all your passwords so you don't have to. You only need to remember one master password. Popular options: Bitwarden (free), 1Password, Google Password Manager.

🚫 Never share your password — not with friends, not with people claiming to be tech support, not with anyone asking for it over phone or email. Legitimate services never ask for your password.
Common Mistakes

Passwords to Absolutely Avoid

Avoid

Personal Info

Your name, birthday, phone number, or city. Attackers look these up on social media first.

Avoid

Sequential Numbers

123456, 111111, abcdef — these are always in attackers' first-attempt lists.

Avoid

Common Words

"password", "iloveyou", "qwerty" — the most used passwords year after year.
