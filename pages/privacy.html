Think Before
You Share

Once something is online, it can be saved, reshared, and found years later. This page covers what to protect, how to spot fake messages, and how to pause under pressure.

Privacy Rules

What Goes Where

Not all information deserves the same level of protection — but some things should never go online at all.

🔴 Never Share

Keep Completely Private

  • OTP (One-Time Password) from SMS
  • Bank account number or PIN
  • Aadhaar or PAN card details
  • Passwords of any account
  • Intimate or private photographs
  • Your home address or school route
🟡 Post with Care

Think Before Posting

  • Check your audience before every post
  • Assume photos can be saved and reshared
  • Review privacy settings monthly
  • Ask: "Would I be OK with this in 5 years?"
🔵 When in Doubt

Pause and Verify

  • Pause and take a breath before reacting
  • Ask a trusted adult if you're unsure
  • Remove sensitive content if pressured
  • Report suspicious requests to authorities
Spot the Scam

How to Identify a Fake Message

Use this checklist to decide whether a message is genuine before you click or reply.

Example scam message:

🎉 Congratulations! You won ₹50,000. Click here immediately to claim your prize.

Here's why this is a scam:

Unknown sender — mismatched or strange email/phone number.
Urgent language — "immediately", "now", "act fast" are red flags.
Suspicious link — shortened URLs hide the real destination.
Too good to be true — you can't win a prize you never entered.
Verify Checklist

4-Step Verification

1

Check the Sender

Look at the full email address or phone number — not just the display name. Fraudsters use addresses like "support@bank-secure-alert.com" instead of your actual bank's domain.

2

Don't Click — Hover or Search

Hover over any link to see the real URL before clicking. Or open the official website directly in your browser by typing the address yourself.

3

Call the Organisation Directly

Use the official phone number from the organisation's website — not a number provided in the suspicious message itself.

4

When in Doubt, Delete

If you can't verify it's genuine, don't engage. Delete the message and block the sender. No legitimate organisation will penalise you for being cautious.

✅ Remember: If unsure, do not click any links — open the official site directly in your browser or contact the organisation. Caution is always the right choice.
