Common Cyber Threats

Cybercriminals actively target young users with social-engineering tactics and fake offers. Knowing what's out there is your first defence.

Why It Matters

You're a Target

Over 5 billion people use the internet. Students who spend hours daily on Instagram, WhatsApp and YouTube are prime targets for scams and manipulation. Understanding the threat landscape is the first step to protection.

🤚 Quick check: How many of you use Instagram, WhatsApp, or YouTube every day? Most of you — which means all of you need to know these threats.
The Four Major Threats

What You're Up Against

🎣
Phishing

Fake Emails & Messages

Fraudulent emails or messages that trick you into revealing passwords or clicking malicious links.

  • Emails claiming your account is suspended
  • Messages pretending to be your bank
  • Fake "verify your email" links
🎁
Online Scams

Fake Offers & Prizes

Fake offers, prizes, or giveaways designed to extract money or personal data from victims.

  • "You've won ₹50,000! Claim now"
  • Too-good-to-be-true job offers
  • Lottery wins you never entered
💬
Cyberbullying

Harassment Online

Harassment, threats, or public shaming that seriously impacts mental health and personal safety.

  • Threatening messages or comments
  • Sharing private photos without consent
  • Group exclusion or coordinated harassment
🪪
Identity Theft

Stolen Identity

Someone uses your personal information without permission to commit fraud or access your accounts.

  • Using your Aadhaar or PAN details
  • Opening accounts in your name
  • Taking loans using your identity
How They Get You

Common Attack Patterns

1

Build Trust First

Attackers often pose as someone you trust — your bank, a government body, or a popular brand — using official-looking logos and language.

2

Create Urgency

"Act now or your account will be blocked." Urgency bypasses rational thinking — it's designed to make you click before you think.

3

Steal or Extort

Once you've clicked or shared information, attackers extract money, credentials, or data — often locking you out of your own accounts.

✅ Key takeaway: If something feels urgent, too good to be true, or asks for personal information — stop. Verify through official channels before acting.
