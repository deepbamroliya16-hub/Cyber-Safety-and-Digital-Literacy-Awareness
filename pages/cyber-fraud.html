If You're Attacked

Quick, decisive steps create the best chance of stopping loss and preserving evidence. This page covers what to do the moment you suspect cyber fraud — and how to recover afterwards.

📞 National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930
🚨

Act Immediately — Every Minute Counts

If money has already left your account or your credentials have been stolen, call 1930 right now. The faster you act, the higher the chance of recovery.

Phase 1: Immediate Action Do This First
1

Call 1930 — Freeze Transactions

Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930 immediately. They can initiate a kill switch that pauses payments across banks and gateways, preventing further money transfer.

2

File an Official Report Online

Submit a complaint at www.cybercrime.gov.in to create an official digital trail for investigators and your bank. Do this alongside calling 1930, not instead of it.

3

Notify Your Bank Immediately

Contact your bank's fraud team to block compromised cards, UPI handles, and accounts. Request transaction monitoring and ask about emergency reversal procedures.

Phase 2: Contain the Damage Do This Next
1

Pause Online Activity

Stop online banking, shopping, and recurring payments until your accounts are secured and verified clean.

2

Change All Passwords

Immediately change passwords for your email, banking, and any accounts using the same credentials. Enable strong, unique passwords and turn on multi-factor authentication.

3

Isolate Infected Devices

Disconnect or power down any device you suspect is compromised. Avoid using it for sensitive actions until it has been professionally cleaned.

💡 If money has already left your account, ask your bank about chargeback options and emergency reversal procedures right away — time windows for these are short.
Phase 3: Recovery Rebuild Securely
1

Audit Your Accounts

Review recent bank and card statements. Check credit reports for new accounts opened in your name. Document all unauthorised charges carefully for insurance and bank claims.

2

Report Widely

File complaints with national and local authorities. Include the helpline report number in all communications. Forward phishing emails to authorities for investigation.

3

Rebuild Securely

Use a password manager for unique passwords on every account. Enable 2FA everywhere. Never allow unsolicited remote access to your device. Keep all software patched and updated.

4

Preserve All Evidence

Save all communications — emails, screenshots, and transaction IDs. These are vital for ongoing investigations and for recovering funds from your bank or insurers.
