Social Media Safety

Instagram, WhatsApp, YouTube — these platforms are where students spend hours daily. How you behave on them shapes your safety, reputation, and digital footprint for years.

The Platforms

Where Risks Live

📸

Instagram

Public posts, story reposts, and fake follower requests are common attack vectors. Keep your profile private.

💬

WhatsApp

Fake forwarded messages spread misinformation rapidly. OTP theft and impersonation scams are most common here.

▶️

YouTube

Fake "prize" comments, malicious links in video descriptions, and phishing via community posts are rising threats.

Behaviour Guide

Do's and Don'ts

✅ Do's
  • Keep accounts set to private — review settings monthly
  • Accept friend or follow requests only from people you know in real life
  • Report suspicious profiles, messages, or comments to the platform
  • Think before you post — consider how it will look in 5 years
  • Enable login notifications so you know if someone else logs in
  • Use strong, unique passwords for each social account
  • Log out of social accounts on shared or school computers
❌ Don'ts
  • Don't share your school name, address, or daily routine publicly
  • Never agree to meet online strangers in person alone
  • Don't click on unknown or unverified links in messages or comments
  • Avoid responding to messages that pressure, intimidate or threaten you
  • Don't share your location in real time on public stories
  • Never forward messages without verifying if they are true
  • Don't share OTPs or passwords — even with someone claiming to be support
If It Happens to You

What to Do About Cyberbullying

1

Don't Respond

Reacting gives the bully attention and power. Step away from the device and do not engage.

2

Document Everything

Take screenshots of the messages, profile, or posts. Preserve the evidence before blocking.

3

Block and Report

Use the platform's built-in report and block features. All major platforms take cyberbullying seriously.

4

Tell a Trusted Adult

Share what happened with a parent, teacher, or school counsellor. You don't have to handle this alone. It's never your fault.

💡 Quick tip: Your digital footprint follows you into college applications and job interviews. Employers and colleges do search candidates online. Post with that in mind.
